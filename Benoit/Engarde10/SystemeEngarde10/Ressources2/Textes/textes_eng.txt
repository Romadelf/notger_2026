#textes_engarde=English/English; English texts of 20/8/2019

This file contains the texts of ENGARDE. It can be translated into another language.
For this:
* modify the first line:
  - keep: "#textes_engarde="
  - write after the name of the language in the language itself then "/"
  - write after the name of the language in English then ";"
  - write after the name of the translator and the date day/month/year
* then translate the lines starting by # 

A line starting with # and containing = describes a text, otherwise it's a comment
which is ignored when loading.
The part between # and = is the symbol that should not be modified.
The part after = is the body of the text to be translated into your language.

For some texts, we also give a reduced version
we use the same symbol with% at the end
example:
normal text: #categorie=catégorie
reduced text: #categorie%=cat

It is recommended to write the text in lower case. ENGARDE takes in charge for uppercase
in general.

******************************************************************************

Somes sentences have places denoted ^1 or ^2 which are replaced by a number or a word by ENGARDE 
example:  #tour_no=tour No ^1

******************************************************************************

In several sentences, ENGARDE manages genders and numbers.
First example: international[/e|s/es]
between [] there are several way to complete the word:
"|" separates numbers: singular|plural
"/" separates gender: male/female

In some sentences, ENGARDE manages genders and numbers related to a place:
example:  #elimine=^1 éliminé[1|/e|s/es]
the gender and number will be this of the word replacing ^1 because of 1| after [

******************************************************************************

The gender of some words is indicated with (f) ou (m) before the word
example:  #competition=(f)compétition[|s]

******************************************************************************

THE SENTENCES TO BE TRANSLATED HAVE A & AFTER =
example: #tour_no=&tour No ^1
It helps to search the sentences to be translated
REMOVE THE & WHEN YOU TRANSLATE A SENTENCE

******************************************************************************

Indicate here if the language uses latine letters (write oui or non)
When non is written, the texts are not modified:
- no transformation in upper/lower case
- no suppression of spaces, no gender or number consideration.
Texts must be written without [ / | ] and without (f) and (m)
but ^ must be kept
